Measures of Central Tendency

Values which locate the centre of a frequency distribution are know as measure of centre tendency. 

The measure of centre tendency is to act as representative.

The most known central tendency measures are:

Arithmetic mean is the sum of all observations divided by the number of observations.

$$\overline{x} = \frac{\sum x_i }{n}$$

If we have a frequency distribution, then the formula is

$$\overline{x} = \frac{\sum {(x_i*n_i)} }{n}$$

Median - the value which divides the distribution into two equal parts (the values should be ordered).

Mode - the value which occurs most frequently.

There are also other central tendency measures, such as:

Midrange - the mid-point of the observations.

The Geometric Mean of a sample of n positive observations is defined as the nth root of the product of the n numbers.

Measures of Dispersion or Variation

Along with measures of central tendency it is important to know something of the variability of the observations.

The most used measures of variations are:

  • Range is the difference between the highest and lowest values.

$$Range = x_{max} - x_{min}$$

  • Average Deviation is the sum of the absolute values (disregard sign) of the deviations of the individual measurements from the median divided by the number of observations.
$$Avg. dev = \frac{| \sum x_i - median |}{n}$$
